LEAD-LAG STRATEGY BACKTESTING REPORT
====================================

1. INTRODUCTION

This project implements a lead-lag based quantitative trading strategy.
The objective of the strategy is to identify whether one asset (leader)
moves before another asset (lagger), and use this relationship to generate
trading signals.

The approach is based on statistical techniques such as correlation analysis
and Granger causality testing. If a leading relationship is detected, signals
are generated to take long or short positions accordingly.

The strategy is evaluated using a backtesting framework to measure its
performance across different timeframes.


================================================================================
2. SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

The system is modular and consists of the following components:

* data/data_cleaning.py:
  Responsible for preprocessing raw stock data, handling missing values,
  and computing log returns.

* strategy/correlation.py:
  Computes cross-correlation between leader and lagger assets and identifies
  the optimal lag where the relationship is strongest.

* strategy/granger.py:
  Performs Granger causality tests to statistically validate whether one
  time series can predict another.

* strategy/signal.py:
  Generates trading signals (BUY, SELL, HOLD) based on lagged relationships.

* backtest/engine.py:
  Executes the backtesting process by simulating trades and tracking performance.

* backtest/performance.py:
  Calculates performance metrics such as returns, Sharpe ratio, drawdown, etc.

This modular design ensures clear separation of concerns and easy extensibility.
